STRATEGY / VOTING / ALLIANCE SYSTEM — REMAINING ROADMAP
========================================================

STATUS
------
The major implementation roadmap is complete.

Implemented:
- Temperament-aware but numbers-conscious rogue voting
- Observable vote commitments and imperfect plan beliefs
- Alliance ballot reliability and eligible-voter math
- Majority, plurality, tie, and no-vote awareness
- Rational idol split votes

- Vote-pitch negotiation with stat, bond, and viability checks

REMAINING WORK
--------------

1. FULL-SEASON INTEGRATION AUDIT

Run many complete seasons instead of isolated tribals and measure:
- Average alliance lifespan
- Alliances formed, dissolved, and re-formed per season
- Average betrayals, exposed pitches, apologies, truces, and expulsions
- Repeat targeting and recurring rivalries
- Idol play accuracy, wasted idols, ally plays, and missed danger
- Reputation labels earned per contestant and per season
- Whether reputations change naturally instead of becoming permanent too early
- Jury outcomes and whether reputation has a reasonable influence
- Frequency of unanimous, majority, plurality, tied, and rock-draw votes
- Whether different archetypes and stat profiles produce visibly different games

Suggested sample size:
- 100–500 complete seasons for automated metrics
- 5–10 manually reviewed seasons for narrative continuity


2. CROSS-SCREEN STORY CONTINUITY REVIEW

For selected episodes, compare this exact sequence:

Camp events
→ Voting Plans
→ Tribal Council ballots
→ Why This Vote Happened
→ Text backlog / debug
→ Next episode’s alliance fallout

Check that:
- Camp hints do not reveal exact ballots prematurely
- Voting Plans accurately describe known numbers and uncertainty
- Late changes have an understandable trigger
- Ballot reasons match the actual plan, bond, memory, or counterplay
- Idol decisions match the holder’s perceived information
- Alliance fallout references the correct plan and betrayal
- Repair scenes occur only when the betrayal was detected
- Reputation changes are supported by repeated visible behavior
- The eliminated-player explanation matches the votes that counted


3. REPETITION AND WRITING CLEANUP

Search for overused wording and events:
- “This vote makes sense”
- “The plan still needs people to hold”
- “No credible late coalition”
- “The heat has been building”
- “This isn’t strategy — it’s honesty”
- Repeated pitch, goat, betrayal, and reputation cards

Add more contextual variants, but do not sacrifice accuracy.
Prefer one consolidated event over several cards describing the same development.


4. EDGE-CASE TESTING

Explicitly test:
- Multiple idols played at the same Tribal
- Idol plus split vote plus Extra Vote
- Shot in the Dark during a negotiated coalition
- Two competing pitches sharing the same voters
- A counter-pitch against an immune organizer
- Pitch target or organizer losing their vote
- No-vote players participating in strategy without adding ballots
- Alliance member expelled before attempting repair
- Alliance dissolving while a repair is pending
- Reputation labels after swaps, returns, and Rescue Island re-entry
- Final 5 / Final 4 vote thresholds
- All votes cancelled
- Multi-tribal and double-elimination episodes
- Save/load in the middle of an episode with pitch or repair information


5. SAVE-STATE AND OLD-SAVE COMPATIBILITY

Verify that older saves without these fields still load:
- strategicMemories
- strategicReputations
- allianceRepairHistory
- strategyExclusions
- pitchIncidents
- votePitches
- pitchIntel
- pitchCounterplay
- pitchAllianceFallout
- allianceRepairs

All missing fields should receive safe defaults.
Test save → reload → continue for at least three episodes.


6. USER-INTERFACE POLISH

Review Voting Plans on desktop and mobile:
- Reputation section should remain compact
- Plan Stability cards should not become too dense
- Explain technical phrases in normal language
- Keep debug-only certainty out of the normal viewer screen
- Make “estimated support” visually distinct from “model-wide forecast”
- Keep eligible voters, ballots, and alliance membership clearly separated
- Consider collapsible advanced detail for reputation percentages


7. FINAL BALANCING

Only tune after the full-season audit.

Watch for:
- Persuasive reputation snowballing into automatic pitch success
- Unreliable or Leaky labels making a player permanently unusable
- Alliances forgiving too easily or never forgiving
- Strategy exclusion lasting too long
- Counterplay reversing too many votes
- Idol holders reading danger too accurately
- Excessive plurality chaos
- Too many unanimous votes
- Reputations appearing after too little evidence

Re-run the realism audit after every meaningful probability change.

RECOMMENDED RESUME ORDER
------------------------
1. Build the full-season audit.
2. Run 100–500 seasons and save the metrics.
3. Manually inspect 5–10 seasons across all viewer screens.
4. Fix contradictions and save-state problems.
5. Reduce repetitive narrative wording.
6. Tune probabilities only where the collected evidence shows a problem.
7. Run the complete regression suite and realism audit again.


DEFINITION OF DONE
------------------
The system is finished when:
- Full seasons remain stable without strategic or narrative contradictions
- Alliances, pitches, betrayals, repairs, idols, and reputations create coherent arcs
- Contestants with different stats produce noticeably different decisions
- Viewer screens explain events without spoiling hidden decisions
- Save/load works with both new and old saves
- Automated metrics remain within healthy ranges
- The full test suite passes
