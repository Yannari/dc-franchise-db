── RELATIONSHIPS ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[B] NON-MUTUAL RELATIONSHIP (one-sided bond)
Trigger: Player A has bond >= 4 with B but B has low loyalty (<= 3) AND
  B is in a rival alliance that doesn't include A
Roll: passive — detected each episode, not a one-time event
What happens: A genuinely trusts B and won't vote them out, but B feels no
  such obligation. The asymmetry grows over time — A makes defensive decisions
  to protect B, B uses A as a shield. Eventually B votes A out and A is
  blindsided because they never saw it coming.
Priority: MEDIUM — high drama but needs an architectural decision first.
Architectural note: current bonds are SYMMETRIC (bKey sorts names, single value
  for both directions). Non-mutual relationships need either:
  Option 1 — Directional bonds: gs.bonds stores A→B and B→A separately.
    Major refactor but unlocks this and many other asymmetric dynamics.
  Option 2 — Perceived bond overlay: gs.perceivedBonds[A][B] = how much A
    THINKS the bond is worth. Doesn't change B's actual loyalty, just A's
    decision-making. Smaller change, achieves the illusion effect.
  Recommend Option 2 as a first pass.
Mechanic (Option 2):
  - Each episode: if A has bond >= 4 with B AND B's loyalty <= 3, there's a
    ~15% chance A's perceived bond overshoots reality by +1 to +2
  - A's vote logic uses perceivedBond(A→B) instead of actual bond for
    willingness-to-vote-B calculations
  - When B votes A out: A's perceivedBond collapses to actual bond — massive
    emotional fallout (camp event next episode, bond drop with B's allies)
  - Generates a camp event when the illusion cracks: "A thought they knew where
    they stood. The vote told a different story."


── CAMP / PRE-TRIBAL ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[1] PARANOIA SPIRAL
Trigger: player with emotional state 'paranoid' + strategic >= 7
Roll: ~15-30% per episode
What happens: player convinces themselves a real ally is plotting against them,
  confronts them or starts quietly campaigning against them — even though the ally
  was loyal. Creates a self-fulfilling prophecy: the accused ally actually flips now.
Mechanic: generates a camp event, forces a bond drop between the two, and nudges
  the accused ally's vote away from the paranoid player's preferred target.

[2] SOCIAL BOMB / FAUX PAS
Trigger: player with temperament <= 3 or boldness >= 8
Roll: ~10-20% per episode
What happens: player says something that lands badly at camp — offensive, arrogant,
  or just tone-deaf. Everyone who witnesses it takes a bond hit with them.
Mechanic: all tribemates take -0.5 to -1.5 bond with that player depending on their
  own social stat (high social = more offended). Camp event generated. Increases
  their vote heat for that tribal.

[3] OVERPLAYING AT TRIBAL
Trigger: player with strategic >= 8 + paranoid or desperate emotional state
Roll: ~20% when they are NOT the top vote-getter
What happens: player starts whispering too much at tribal council, makes side deals
  visible to everyone, alerts the majority. Votes switch onto them mid-tribal.
Mechanic: a portion of votes that were going to the original target redirect to the
  overplayer. Can cause an unexpected boot. Generates a WHY THIS VOTE HAPPENED note.

[4] INFORMATION BROKER
Trigger: player with social >= 8 + loyalty <= 4, game has 2 active named alliances
Roll: ~25% once per game (not per episode)
What happens: player starts feeding intel to both sides simultaneously. While active,
  they gain a "broker" status flag that boosts their social standing but increases
  betrayal risk. The broker gets exposed when both alliances compare notes — usually
  at a merge or swap — causing a massive bond collapse with everyone who trusted them.
Mechanic: flag persists across episodes, escalating exposure risk each ep. Blowup
  generates a camp event and tanks bonds with both alliances at once.

[5] STOLEN CREDIT
Trigger: a "big move" vote happens (underdog wins, alliance crack, blindside)
Roll: ~20% if there's a player with boldness >= 8 who wasn't the orchestrator
What happens: the bold player takes loud public credit for the move at camp or in
  confessional, overshadowing the actual architect. Architect takes a resentment hit.
  Jury respect shifts toward the credit-stealer even though they didn't do the work.
Mechanic: bond hit between orchestrator and credit-stealer. Jury perception adjusted.
  Generates a camp event. Relevant at FTC if both are finalists.

[6] FALSE IDOL PLANT
Trigger: player with strategic >= 8 is actively being targeted AND an idol search is in progress
Roll: ~15%
What happens: player crafts a fake idol and plants it in an obvious location.
  A searching player finds it and believes it's real. If they bring it to tribal and
  play it, it's revealed as fake — humiliating and they lose credibility.
Mechanic: gs.fakeIdols[] entry created. If played at tribal: no votes negated,
  a camp event generated next episode, bond collapse between fake-idol-holder and everyone.

[17] VOLUNTEER EXILE DUEL — ASK TO BE ELIMINATED
Trigger: gs.exilePlayer is set (someone is already waiting at 2nd Chance Isle),

What happens: a player decides their best shot at eliminating a rival is to go

  toward themselves, staying silent when they could campaign, or outright telling
  a trusted ally "send me, I'll beat them." They're voted out deliberately, face

  context ("This wasn't an accident — [Name] came here looking for [Exile].").
  If the volunteer loses the duel, it's an especially bitter exit (boldness >= 7
  triggers a blowup scene). If they win, their return carries outsized respect
  and narrative weight. Camp event on their return: "They chose this."
Priority: MEDIUM — needs exile duel to be active and gs.exilePlayer check. Clean
  to add once exile infrastructure is stable.

[9] TRIBAL COUNCIL BLOWUP

── SIMULATION IMPROVEMENTS ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[16] CUMULATIVE CHALLENGE PERFORMANCE
Trigger: any player who has won or placed top-2 in 2+ challenges
Roll: passive — recalculated each episode, no single fire moment
What happens: challenge performance accumulates in gs.chalRecord[playerName]
  tracking wins, top-2 finishes, and bottom-2 finishes across all episodes.
  A player on a win streak becomes a progressively larger target — not because
  of their stats, but because of what the tribe has witnessed. A puzzle player
  with 3 wins is just as scary as a physical beast. A player with 3 bottom-2
  finishes gets quietly dismissed as a non-threat.
Mechanic:
  - gs.chalRecord[name] = { wins: 0, podiums: 0, bombs: 0 } — incremented
    after each episode's challenge resolves.
  - threatScore(name) gains a chalPrestige modifier:
      wins >= 3 → +1.5
      wins >= 2 → +0.8
      podiums >= 3 → +0.5
      bombs >= 3 → -0.5 (dismissed, easier to keep as goat)
  - Summary text gets a line in POST-CHALLENGE STATUS if a player crosses a
    threshold: "NOTE: [Name] has now won 3 challenges. The tribe is watching."
  - camp event pool gets a new type 'chal-threat' that fires ~30% when a player
    crosses wins >= 2: someone names them at camp as a challenge threat.
Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH — affects every episode from ep 3+ onward. Gives
  puzzle/mental players the same long-term danger arc as physical beasts.
  Clean to implement; no new event types needed beyond the record tracking.


[18] COMFORT BLINDSPOT
Trigger: player in 'comfortable' emotional state (already tracked in gs.playerStates)
  who is also the top vote-getter this tribal. The 'comfortable' state already exists
  in updatePlayerStates — it fires when a player has been safe for 5+ episodes AND
  has low boldness (< 5) AND low strategic (< 6). Currently it does nothing mechanically.
  This idea gives it teeth.
Roll: passive — no roll needed. If the player IS comfortable AND is the top target,
  the blindspot suppresses their normal survival instincts automatically.
What happens: they walk into tribal without reading the room. They don't scramble,
  don't pick up on signals, don't use their idol if they have one. They vote whoever
  they planned to vote days ago, not whoever is actually threatening them right now.
  They get eliminated without any defensive play. The narrative reads as a blindside
  even if the vote wasn't particularly complex — their comfort was the weapon used
  against them.
Mechanic:
  - In simulateVotes: comfortable players skip the intuition-based preemptive strike
    check entirely (already true — that check requires paranoid/desperate). But also:
    comfortable players don't react to computeHeat — their vote target locks to their
    alliance's stated plan even if the heat is pointing back at them.
  - In checkIdolPlays: if the comfort player holds an idol, suppress the auto-play
    threshold by ~40% — they don't feel threatened enough to use it.
    (Currently idol play willingness scales with emotional state; comfortable = low
    urgency, so raise the willingness threshold so the idol stays in pocket.)
  - Camp event the episode BEFORE: ~25% chance someone notices the comfortable player
    is "not paying attention" and uses it. Generates a pre-tribal event: "[Name] hasn't
    seemed worried about anything in weeks. [Other] clocked it." This seeds the
    blindside in the previous episode's camp narrative.
  - VP display: if a comfortable player is eliminated, add a note to the Tribal Council
    screen — something like "They never saw it coming. Three weeks of safety had made
    them invisible to their own danger."
  - Idol suppression only applies if boldness < 6 AND strategic < 6. A bold comfortable
    player still plays their idol out of ego — low strategic is the key variable.
Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH — no new infrastructure needed. The 'comfortable' state already
  tracks the right conditions. This just activates the "blind spot" comment that's been
  sitting in updatePlayerStates with no mechanic behind it.
Implementation note: the comfortable state currently gives emotionalMod = 0 in vote
  logic. That stays. The new behavior is suppression (don't react to heat, don't
  auto-play idol) rather than a numeric penalty — which is actually more correct to
  the drama. A paranoid player at least TRIES to survive. A comfortable player doesn't
  try because they don't know they need to.


[19] ALLIANCE RECRUITMENT
Trigger: one of three scenarios each episode:
  A) Post-tribe-swap — a player is now a minority outsider on their new tribe AND
     a named alliance has >= 2 members on that tribe with avg bond >= 3 with the outsider.
  B) A player just quit a named alliance (checkAllianceQuitting fired) AND has
     bond >= 3 with at least one member of a different active named alliance.
  C) A blindside just happened (eliminated player was in a named alliance) AND
     a swing voter who voted with the winning side has bond >= 3 with that winning bloc.
Roll: ~30-45% per qualifying scenario per episode (higher for scenario A — swap
  outsiders are under immediate pressure to find protection).
What happens: the recruiting alliance pulls the target player in. Their name is
  added to alliance.members. Bonds between the recruit and existing members get a
  +0.5 boost. A camp event fires: "[Recruiter] brought [Player] into the fold —
  [Alliance] just got bigger." The recruit's emotional state shifts toward 'confident'.
  If the recruit was previously in a rival alliance, that alliance takes a trust hit
  and the recruit is flagged as a betrayal risk (loyalty check applied next tribal).
Mechanic:
  - checkAllianceRecruitment(ep) runs after checkAllianceQuitting each episode.
  - Iterate gs.namedAlliances filtering for active ones with 2+ active members.
  - For each alliance, check qualifying players (not already members, active, present).
  - Score candidates: bond avg with current members + social + loyalty (inverse —
    low loyalty = higher recruitment appeal since they're easier to pull).
  - Pick the best candidate, roll, push them into alliance.members if fires.
  - Log to camp events. Bond boost. Emotional state update.
  - Prevent double-recruitment: a player can only be recruited once per episode.
  - Cap alliance size: don't recruit if alliance already has >= 5 active members
    (prevents one alliance from absorbing everyone).
Priority: HIGH — this is a gap in the current architecture. Alliances can only
  shrink (quitting, betrayal, elimination) but never grow. Post-swap outsiders have
  no path to safety. Swing voters who flip have no way to formalize their new loyalty.
  Clean to implement: checkAllianceRecruitment is a new function that reads/writes
  gs.namedAlliances and fires a camp event. No new state keys needed beyond
  what already exists.
Implementation note: scenario A requires knowing the pre-swap tribe. Use
  ep.tribesAtStart to identify who moved tribes this episode (compare with current
  gs.tribes). Scenario B requires checkAllianceQuitting to set a flag on the
  player (e.g., gs.recentlyQuit = Set) that checkAllianceRecruitment can read.


── ADVANTAGES ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

[20] SUPER IDOL (plays AFTER the votes are read)
Trigger: holder is the top vote-getter at tribal, votes have been read
Roll: auto-plays (holder would never choose not to use it when they're going home)
What happens: unlike a normal idol (played before votes are read — guesswork),
  the Super Idol activates AFTER votes are revealed. The holder sees the votes
  land on them and then plays it — guaranteed save, zero risk of misplay.
  This makes it the most powerful advantage in the game. Finding it should be
  extremely rare and it should come with a visible cost so the tribe can
  counterplay.
Mechanic:
  - New advantage type: 'superIdol' in ADVANTAGES array
  - checkIdolPlays currently runs BEFORE resolveVotes. Super Idol needs a
    second pass AFTER resolveVotes: if the eliminated player holds a superIdol,
    negate all votes against them, remove from elimination, consume the idol.
  - Resolution order: normal idols (pre-vote) → votes resolved → Super Idol
    (post-vote) → Second Life Amulet (post-vote, duel).
  - Super Idol fires before Second Life Amulet — if you have both, the Super
    Idol saves you cleanly (no duel needed).
Tradeoffs (pick 1-2 to keep it balanced):
  A) Publicly known: when found, the ENTIRE tribe is told someone found it.
     Everyone knows it exists — they just don't know who has it. Creates
     paranoia, split votes, and flush attempts. The holder can't hide it.
  B) Expires fast: only lasts 2-3 tribals after finding it. Use it or lose it.
     Prevents hoarding. Creates a visible countdown that the tribe can track.
  C) Vote penalty: holder loses their vote at every tribal while holding it
     (like Beware but permanent until played). The safety net costs influence.
  D) Transferable on elimination: if the holder is voted out and doesn't
     use it (not enough votes, or they chose not to), it passes to their
     closest ally — extending the paranoia.
  E) Can't be played for allies: self-only. No ally-play option unlike the
     normal idol. The selfishness of the advantage is part of its design.
Discovery:
  - Camp search: extremely rare (~0.1% base, lower than any other advantage)
  - Exile Island: rare find tier (~3%)
  - Auction: possible blind bid item (~8% if enabled)
  - Three Gifts: possible third gift option
  - NOT findable via Journey (too powerful for a side game reward)
VP Display:
  - Vote reveal: all votes land, elimination name shown, THEN the Super Idol
    reveal fires — portrait + gold badge "SUPER IDOL" + all votes wiped.
    The most dramatic moment possible: the tribe thinks they got their target,
    and then the rug is pulled.
  - Camp event on find: "Something is different about [Name]. They came back
    from the well with a look that said more than any confession could."
  - If publicly known (tradeoff A): every tribal from that point shows a
    small indicator "[Name] holds the Super Idol" in Voting Plans, and the
    tribe's targeting shifts to flush it via split votes.
Priority: MEDIUM — powerful addition but needs careful balancing. The tradeoff
  choice determines whether it's a fun dramatic tool or a game-breaking weapon.
  Recommend tradeoffs A+B (publicly known + expires) for maximum strategic
  tension with minimal balance risk.

Core: per-player survival score decaying each episode. Low survival causes

[22] SHOWMANCE LIFECYCLE
Trigger: existing showmance detection (bond >= 5 + compatible archetypes) already fires once.
  This expands it into a multi-episode arc with escalating drama and strategic consequences.
What happens: showmances evolve through distinct phases that create camp events, influence
  votes, paint targets, and eventually force a dramatic choice.

Phases:
  1. SPARK (current, already implemented)
     - Detected when bond crosses threshold + archetype compatibility
     - One-off camp event announcing the connection
     - Showmance flag set on gs.showmances

  2. HONEYMOON (episodes 1-3 after spark)
     - Pair gravitates toward each other in camp events more often
     - Bond growth accelerated between them (+0.3 bonus per episode)
     - Camp events: stolen glances, inside jokes, late-night conversations
     - Tribe starts noticing — "everyone sees it except them" narrative
     - The pair defends each other in conversations (loyalty boost in votes)

  3. TARGET ON THEIR BACK (episode 3+ after spark)
     - Strategic players flag them as a power couple
     - computeHeat: +1.5 pair heat for showmance partners (already partially exists)
     - Camp events: someone proposes splitting them up, alliance discussions shift
     - The pair becomes a voting bloc that others have to account for
     - Confessional: "Those two are going to the end together if we don't do something"

  4. JEALOUSY / THIRD WHEEL (~20% per episode when a 3rd player has bond >= 3 with one partner)
     - A player close to one half of the showmance feels excluded
     - Bond drop with the pair (-0.5 to -1.0)
     - Camp event: "Something changed. They don't need me anymore."
     - The jealous player becomes a potential swing vote against the couple
     - Creates a triangle that adds narrative texture beyond simple pair dynamics

  5. THE TEST (when one partner is targeted at tribal)
     - The other partner faces a choice: vote with the alliance or protect them
     - Loyalty threshold boosted by showmance bond — they're more likely to defy the plan
     - If they break ranks to save the partner: alliance fallout, dramatic narrative
     - If they vote with the alliance: showmance takes a massive hit, potential breakup
     - Camp event foreshadows: "Tonight is going to test everything"

  6. RESOLUTION — breakup or ride-or-die
     - BREAKUP: one votes out the other → bond collapses to -5 or lower
       - Biggest betrayal event in the game
       - Camp events next episode: grief, anger, tribe processing what happened
       - Jury impact: if both make FTC, the breakup dominates the narrative
     - RIDE-OR-DIE: both survive together past episode 5 of the showmance
       - Bond becomes functionally unbreakable (+8 or higher)
       - They vote as a unit — effectively a permanent alliance
       - The tribe either accepts them as a package deal or targets them harder

Mechanic:
