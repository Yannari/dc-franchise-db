[DR-1] THE CLASS TRIAL (Danganronpa-inspired twist)
  Trigger: schedulable, post-merge, one episode.
  Mechanic: One player randomly chosen as "The Blackened" — commits a camp crime
    (steals food, hides advantage, sabotages supplies). Tribe investigates via
    stat-gated Truth Bullet events (intuition * 0.06, mental * 0.04, social * 0.05).
    The Blackened plants Red Herrings (strategic * 0.05). Low-temperament Blackened
    may crack under pressure. At tribal, a Class Trial replaces the normal vote —
    players vote on who they think is guilty based on evidence scores, not alliances.
  Outcomes:
    Correct (majority identifies Blackened): EXECUTION — Blackened eliminated, crime
      reversed, detective gets bond + popularity boost, tribe trust restored.
    Wrong (majority picks innocent): WRONGFUL CONVICTION — innocent eliminated,
      Blackened gets immunity next ep, crime stays, trust damage -0.5 across tribe,
      next-ep reveal event.
  VP: dedicated screens for The Crime, Investigation (TRUTH BULLET / MISLEADING badges),
    Class Trial (accusation/defense/closing argument), The Verdict (EXECUTION or
    WRONGFUL CONVICTION), The Truth (viewer-only Blackened reveal if wrong).
  Note: complex — evidence system, trial vote mechanics, 6 new camp event types.
    Compatible with The Mole (Mole can be Blackened). Incompatible with no-tribal.

[TD-7] CHALLENGE CHEATING
  Problem: only the Mole sabotages challenges. Regular players should cheat to WIN.
  Mechanic: bold + low-loyalty players can cheat during challenges (extra time, stealing
    pieces, sabotaging opponent equipment). Proportional: boldness * 0.02 + (10-loyalty) * 0.015.
  Detection: high-intuition witnesses roll to catch the cheater (intuition * 0.04).
  Caught: -1.5 bond with witnesses, heat spike, camp drama event. Disqualified from challenge.
  Uncaught: challenge score boosted +2 to +4. The tribe suspects but can't prove it.
  Camp events: caught-cheating confrontation, suspicious-performance whispers, denial.
  Note: different from Mole (cheating to WIN, not to lose). Once per player per season.

[TD-10] CHAIN OF COMMAND
  Trigger: schedulable, post-merge only. Replaces normal vote.
  Mechanic: immunity winner starts a chain by picking someone safe. That person picks
    the next, and so on. Players can only pick those not yet chosen. Last person
    unpicked is eliminated. No vote — pure social selection.
  Targeting: each pick is bond-weighted (wRandom on perceived bond + alliance membership).
    Early picks = close allies. Middle picks = the awkward "who do I save?" zone.
    Last pick = devastating public rejection.
  Consequences: creates a PUBLIC pecking order record. Late picks cause bond damage
    ("you picked me 7th out of 8"). Early picks confirm loyalty. Camp events next
    episode: confrontation about pick order, gratitude for early picks, resentment
    for late picks.
  VP: dedicated screen showing the chain forming one by one (click-to-reveal). Each
    pick shows portrait + who picked them + reaction. Last person gets elimination card.
  Note: best at 8-10 players. Too short at F5. Entirely social — no stats in the chain,
    just relationships. The immunity winner has massive power (sets the tone for who's
    valued and who's expendable).

[TD-8] FEAR FACTOR CHALLENGE
  Trigger: schedulable twist, overrides immunity challenge. Pre-merge AND post-merge versions.
  Mechanic: challenge is gated by temperament/boldness. Each round, players face something
    scary/disgusting. They can REFUSE — refusing = automatic last place / sit out.
  Pre-merge: tribe challenge format. Players who refuse weaken their tribe. Tribe with
    most refusals loses. Creates blame events against refusers.
  Post-merge: individual format. Refusers are out. Last person standing wins immunity.
    Creates drama when a physical threat refuses and goes home vulnerable.
  Refuse chance: (10 - temperament) * 0.06 + (10 - boldness) * 0.04. High-temperament
    high-boldness players almost never refuse. Low-both players bail constantly.
  Events: refusal shame, "I can't do this" moment, clutch completion, tribe pressure.
  Note: overrides normal challenge for the episode. Needs pre-merge tribe format +
    post-merge individual format.
