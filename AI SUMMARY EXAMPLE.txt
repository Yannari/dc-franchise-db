## PRE-CHALLENGE STATUS

### The Goliath Cold War
The Goliaths returned to camp with their numbers nearly intact, but the social fabric was beginning to tear. The rivalry between **Priya** and **Scott** (Season 4/9 rivals) escalated from mutual dislike to **Extreme Hatred**. Despite this, the tribe's structural integrity remained high due to **Bowie’s** intricate web of alliances. **Zoey** solidified her position as the tribe's social glue, developing **Strong Bonds with both Carrie and Bowie**. The **Mickey and Ryan** (Season 5) duo remained the "Unbreakable" center of the tribe, but their dominance was starting to attract quiet scrutiny from the outskirts.

### The David Paranoia
The David beach was a powder keg of unresolved friction. **Josee** continued her abrasive social game, but she found an unexpected ally in **Stephanie**, establishing a **Small Bond**. Meanwhile, **Brick** was becoming increasingly obsessed with **Hicks**. Drawing on Hicks’s Season 9 reputation as the "Decapitated King" who saw everything, Brick became convinced that Hicks was a sleeper threat. However, Brick’s military intensity was beginning to grate on **Courtney** and **Jasmine**, who viewed his fixation as a distraction from the tribe's desperate need for a win.

---

## ANALYZE BEFORE IMMUNITY

The Davids entered the day knowing that another loss would likely mean the end of their original core. **Stephanie** remained the most dangerous variable on the beach, still holding the **David Tribe Hidden Immunity Idol**. On the Goliath side, **Sanders** (Season 8 winner) continued her stealthy gameplay, keeping her own **Hidden Idol** secret while allowing Bowie and Mickey to take the lead on tribal strategy.

---

## IMMUNITY CHALLENGE: Hunt and Hunted

**The Challenge:** A tactical game of paintball. One team dresses as deer and must avoid being hit by the hunters of the opposing team.
**Winner:** Goliath Tribe
**Reward:** Safety.

**Strategic Narrative:** The Goliaths’ physical coordination was once again superior. **Ryan** and **Scott** acted as elite hunters, while **Carrie** and **Mickey** proved surprisingly elusive as the "deer." The Davids struggled with internal communication; **Courtney** and **Mary** had a visible disagreement during the hunt, leading to their team being picked off one by one. The Goliaths secured their third immunity win, sending the Davids back to Tribal Council for the third time in four episodes.



---

## POST-CHALLENGE STATUS

### 1. Goliath Infighting
Victory did not bring peace to the Goliaths. **Mickey** had a catastrophic afternoon, engaging in **Major Fights with both Cody and Priya**. These conflicts effectively shattered the "Winners' Circle" harmony. In response, **Cody and Priya formed Goliath Tribe Alliance #4**, a defensive pact designed to counteract the Mickey/Ryan power bloc. **Scott and Priya** also had a **Small Fight**, further cementing their status as the tribe's most bitter enemies.

### 2. The David Collapse
On the David beach, the social structure disintegrated. **Josee and Lorenzo engaged in a Major Fight**, while **Courtney and Mary** had their own **Major Fight** over the challenge failure. Amidst this chaos, **Brick** doubled down on his campaign against **Hicks**, trying to convince anyone who would listen that Hicks was a master manipulator. **Courtney**, however, decided that Brick’s erratic behavior and inability to build social bridges made him a "Social Liability."

---

## TRIBAL COUNCIL / VOTE ANALYSIS

**The Vote: The Soldier’s Discharge**
The Davids arrived at Tribal Council in a state of total dysfunction. **Stephanie**, sensing that the tribe had turned on its "Leader," once again chose to hold her **Hidden Immunity Idol**.

**Vote Breakdown: 7-1 (Brick Eliminated)**
* **Votes for Brick (7):** Courtney, Hicks, Jasmine, Josee, Lorenzo, Mary, Stephanie.
* **Votes for Hicks (1):** Brick.

**WHY THIS VOTE HAPPENED:**
**Brick** is gone because he prioritized "The Mission" over "The Men." His absolute conviction that **Hicks** was a threat—based largely on Hicks’s Season 9 history—backfired. To the rest of the Davids, Hicks was a quiet, non-threatening teammate, whereas Brick was a loud, intense presence who was making camp life difficult. **Courtney** orchestrated the vote, convincing the others that Brick was a social liability who was "insane" to be targeting Hicks so early. Brick was discharged by his own tribe for being a "General without an Army."

---

## ELIMINATED
Brick

Reason label: **"The Obsessive Strategist."** He focused so hard on the "Goliath" threat of Hicks that he failed to see the mutiny happening in his own barracks.

## STRATEGIC ANALYSIS

### 1. Hicks: The Invisible Survivor
**Hicks** has survived being a target for three out of four episodes. By outlasting Tyler, Sam, and now Brick, he has proven to be the ultimate "Ghost" of the David tribe. However, Brick’s warnings may have planted a seed of doubt in the minds of Courtney and Mary for the long term.

### 2. Courtney’s David Hegemony
**Courtney** has now dictated the outcome of every David Tribal Council. She is the undisputed leader of the underdogs, successfully maneuvering around Josee’s volatility and Brick’s intensity. Her decision to keep Hicks suggests she values predictable numbers over high-maintenance "Alpha" allies.

### 3. Mickey’s Growing Target
On the Goliath beach, **Mickey** is playing a dangerous game. By fighting with Cody and Priya, he has forced them into an alliance (#4) against him. While he still has his **Unbreakable** bond with Ryan, he is starting to lose the "Social Shield" that helped him win Season 5.

### 4. Stephanie’s Idle Idol
**Stephanie** is now in the Final 16 with a **Hidden Immunity Idol**. She has navigated the early David bloodbath perfectly, staying out of the primary line of fire while holding the most powerful weapon on her beach.
