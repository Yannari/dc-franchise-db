[TD-1] SOLE VOTE (inspired by Total Drama: Grand Chef Auto — Scott's Sole Vote)
  One-shot advantage: holder casts the ONLY valid vote at tribal. All other votes cancelled.
  Trigger: holder plays it at tribal (heat-based play chance, like Vote Block).
  Mechanic: nuclear Vote Block — every other player added to gs.lostVotes. Holder's single
    vote determines the elimination. No split votes, no alliances, no counter-play.
  Consequences: massive bond damage from everyone whose vote was silenced (-0.5 to -1.0
    proportional to bond with holder). Target has no defense except idols.
  Heat: known holder gets +1.5 (everyone wants it flushed — it's terrifying).
  Discovery: rare, same as Vote Block pipeline. One per season max.
  VP: dramatic "SOLE VOTE" banner, all other vote cards greyed out.
  Camp lifecycle: confession/leak/snoop like Vote Block but higher drama.
  Note: works as a new advantage type in the existing ADVANTAGES system.

[15] FINAL TRIBAL SURPRISE FLIP
Trigger: FTC with jury where one finalist has majority of votes locked in
Roll: ~15% if locked-in finalist has at least one jury member with bond <= 0
What happens: a jury member who seemed locked in has a change of heart based on
  final speeches. One previously-certain vote flips.
Mechanic: final vote resolution re-rolls one borderline juror's vote. Only fires
  when the margin is narrow enough to matter.

[B] NON-MUTUAL RELATIONSHIP (one-sided bond)
Trigger: Player A has bond >= 4 with B but B has low loyalty (<= 3) AND
  B is in a rival alliance that doesn't include A
Roll: passive — detected each episode
What happens: A genuinely trusts B and won't vote them out, but B feels no
  such obligation. Eventually B votes A out and A is blindsided.
Architectural note: recommend perceived bond overlay (gs.perceivedBonds) as first pass.
  Smaller change than full directional bonds.

[TD-3] TRIBE RAID
  Trigger: schedulable twist, post-challenge (pre-merge only).
  What happens: winning tribe sends 1-2 raiders to the losing tribe's camp. They take
    one item: food supply, comfort item, or tool. Losing tribe loses the resource.
  Mechanic: raider selection weighted by boldness + physical. Item choice: strategic
    players take food (hurts survival), bold players take comfort (demoralizing), social
    players take tools (sabotage). Losing tribe bond damage toward raiders (-1.0 to -1.5).
    Winning tribe bond boost with raider (+0.3). Creates cross-tribal grudges that pay
    off at merge — "you raided our camp, I remember that."
  Consequence: survival impact (food loss → hunger events), morale damage, and specific
    player-to-player grudges. The raider becomes a known face and a target.
  VP: raider arrives at losing camp, dramatic item selection, losing tribe reaction.
  Note: simple, high drama, Total Drama energy. Works with survival mechanics system.

[TD-4] CHOOSE YOUR CHAMPION
  Trigger: schedulable twist, replaces normal tribe challenge (pre-merge only).
  What happens: each tribe picks one player to compete in a 1v1 challenge. Winner's
    tribe wins immunity. The champion selection itself creates drama.
  Mechanic: tribe selection weighted by physical/endurance/mental (depends on challenge
    type). Selection drama: the tribe discusses and picks — players NOT picked may feel
    overlooked (bond damage if social >= 7 and not chosen). The chosen champion gets
    pressure: if they win, +0.5 bond with entire tribe + hero moment. If they lose,
    challenge liability heat + bond damage from tribe (-0.3 to -0.5).
  Consequence: forces the tribe to publicly evaluate who their best competitor is.
    The champion becomes a visible target post-merge ("they're the one the tribe trusted
    to win"). Creates "why didn't you pick me?" drama and pressure on the selected player.
  VP: champion selection scene, 1v1 challenge result, tribe reaction (celebration or blame).
  Note: simple 1v1 variant of existing challenge system. Clean implementation.

[TD-5] SCHOOLYARD PICK
  Trigger: schedulable twist, alternative to random tribe swap (pre-merge only).
  What happens: two captains are chosen (highest strategic + social). They alternate
    picking players for their team. Last person picked knows they're on the bottom.
  Mechanic: captain selection weighted by strategic * 0.5 + social * 0.5. Captains pick
    in alternating order, choosing players weighted by challenge strength + bond with captain.
    Pick order tracked: early picks get +0.3 bond with captain (valued), last 1-2 picks get
    -0.5 bond (rejected) + emotional state shift to 'uneasy'. The "last picked" player
    becomes a known bottom-dweller — targeted or recruited as a desperate swing vote.
  Consequence: public pecking order established immediately. Creates clear in-group/out-group
    dynamics. Last-picked players become either easy targets or dangerous free agents.
  VP: draft screen showing pick order, captain deliberation, last-picked reaction.
  Note: overlaps with tribe-swap but adds social drama layer. Lower priority than Raid/Champion.

[DC-2] EMISSARY VOTE (Disventure Camp twist)
  Trigger: schedulable twist, post-immunity challenge (pre-merge only).
  What happens: the winning tribe sends one member as an "emissary" to the losing
    tribe's tribal council. The emissary casts a SOLE VOTE to eliminate an additional
    contestant — a double elimination where the tribe votes out one person normally,
    and the emissary eliminates a second person with one vote.
  Mechanic: winning tribe picks emissary (strategic/social weighted, or volunteered).
    Emissary attends losing tribe's tribal as an observer, then casts one decisive vote.
    Emissary's target selection: bonds (cross-tribal ally protection vs enemy targeting),
    strategic read (eliminate the biggest threat), or instructions from their tribe.
    Creates cross-tribal dynamics: the emissary can protect an ally on the other tribe
    (like Gabby protecting Ellie) or target an enemy.
  Consequence: double elimination — one by tribal vote, one by emissary sole vote.
    The losing tribe has no control over who the emissary picks. Creates massive
    cross-tribal strategy, alliances that span tribe lines, and resentment.
    The emissary's choice reveals their cross-tribal loyalties publicly.
  VP: dedicated screen — tribal vote first (normal), then emissary reveal ("But wait —
    tonight's tribal isn't over"). Emissary steps forward, casts their vote, dramatic
    reveal of who they chose and why.
  Bond consequences: emissary gains bond with whoever they protect (ally on other tribe),
    loses bond with the eliminated player's allies. The eliminated player knows exactly
    who ended their game — one person, one vote, no hiding.
  Note: inspired by Disventure Camp 1. Works best as a one-off or rare twist, not
    every episode. Can create pre-tribal deal-making across tribe lines.

[KL-2] TIED DESTINIES (Koh-Lanta twist)
  Trigger: post-merge, final 8/10/12. Players randomly paired for the episode.
  What happens: pairs compete together in challenges. At tribal, the person who
    receives the most votes causes their partner's elimination too (double elim).
  Mechanic: schedulable twist. Random pairing at episode start. Challenge scores
    averaged between pairs. Vote targeting considers both members of the pair.
    Two eliminations in one tribal — the voted-out player AND their tied partner.
  Consequence: massive strategic upheaval. Allies paired with enemies. Bond dynamics.
  Note: complex — needs pair-aware voting, double elimination path, VP display.

[TD-2] THE MOLE (inspired by Total Drama: Rock n' Rule — Owen as the Mole)
  Season-long role: one player is secretly a saboteur. They throw challenges (intentional
    underperformance beyond the existing throw mechanic — structured, not optional) and
    leak alliance information to production / an outside force.
  Trigger: assigned at season start (config toggle). Random or hand-picked player.
  Mechanic: mole has forced challenge throw each episode (detection risk escalates like
    existing throw system). Mole leaks one alliance's target to a random outsider each
    episode (creates "how did they know?" paranoia). Exposure arc: intuition-based
    detection by tribemates, proportional. On exposure: massive bond collapse, potential
    immediate elimination or social pariah status.
  Consequence: season-long tension. Challenges feel unreliable. Information leaks create
    paranoia that feeds into the existing paranoia spiral system.
  Note: complex — season-long role, needs mole assignment UI, forced throws, info leak
    pipeline, exposure arc. Future "season archetype" feature, not a schedulable twist.

[STAT] MENTAL STAT — NON-CHALLENGE ROLE
  Problem: mental is almost entirely a challenge stat (puzzle/balance/mixed). Physical and
    endurance have survival mechanics (provider ability, injury resistance, collapse) as a
    second system. Mental has nothing equivalent outside challenges + vote miscommunication.
  Ideas for expanding mental's social/strategic footprint:
  - Emotional resilience: mental scales resistance to negative emotional state transitions
    (high mental = harder to become paranoid/desperate, recovers to content/calculating faster)
  - Camp strategy sessions: mental improves quality of eavesdrop/intel events (better info)
  - Advantage reading: mental helps identify fake idols (detection chance proportional to mental)
  - Pressure handling: mental reduces bond damage from being on the bottom (stays composed)
  - Tribal focus: high mental reduces vote miscommunication (already done, proportional)
  Note: don't overload mental — it should remain primarily a challenge stat with 1-2 light
    social touches, not become a second strategic stat.
