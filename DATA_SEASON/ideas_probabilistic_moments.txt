PROBABILISTIC MOMENTS — BACKLOG
================================
Ideas for future simulator events. Each entry has a trigger, a probability driver,
and the downstream consequence. None are guaranteed — all fire on a roll.

[DONE] #4 Information Broker — implemented via checkInformationBroker(ep).
  Trigger: player in 2+ active alliances, social >= 5, loyalty <= 5. Once per game.
  Roll: social * 0.025 * (6 - loyalty) * 0.1. Three code paths: activation, active effects, exposure.
  Active: +0.15 bond/ep with both alliances, eavesdrop intel, -0.5 heat. Camp events: brokerWhisper,
  brokerManipulate, brokerConfidence, brokerClose (60%/ep, gold "Double Agent" badge).
  Exposure risk: episodesActive * 0.08, merge/swap +0.30, cap 85%. Detector = highest intuition
  among alliance members (named in narrative). Bond collapse: -(1.0 + episodesActive * 0.4).
  Light -0.3 between alliance members. Exposer gets +0.5 bond with everyone. +2.0 heat for 2 eps.
  Exposure events: brokerExposed (confrontation), brokerFallout, brokerDefense (personality-driven).
  gs.broker = { player, alliances, startEp, episodesActive, exposed, exposedEp, exposer }.

  Roll: (5 - mental) * 0.025 + (4 - social) * 0.03 (~5-15%).
  Wrong target: if split vote active, votes the wrong half. Else: personal grudge (bond <= -2),

  Assignment: majority on primary, bond-protected (bond >= 3 with secondary → primary group).
  Betrayal exempt: voting the split secondary is NOT a betrayal in decayAllianceTrust.

[10] THE GOAT MUTINY
Trigger: 2 players both identified as "goats" (archetype = goat OR threat score <= 2)
  are on the same tribe/merge group, ep >= 8
Roll: ~20% once per game
What happens: the two goats realize they're being dragged along and quietly decide
  to blow up the game. They form a surprise alliance, pull in a swing vote, and take
  out a top player. Bond between the two goats maxes out. Everyone else is rattled.
Mechanic: forced allianceForm event between the two goats, boosted vote heat on the
  highest-threat player that tribal.

[5] STOLEN CREDIT
Trigger: a "big move" vote happens (underdog wins, alliance crack, blindside)
Roll: ~20% if there's a player with boldness >= 8 who wasn't the orchestrator
What happens: the bold player takes loud public credit for the move at camp or in
  confessional, overshadowing the actual architect. Architect takes a resentment hit.
  Jury respect shifts toward the credit-stealer even though they didn't do the work.
Mechanic: bond hit between orchestrator and credit-stealer. Jury perception adjusted.

[KL-1] AMBASSADORS (Koh-Lanta twist)
  Trigger: immediately before the merge, each tribe names one ambassador
  What happens: ambassadors meet privately and must agree on one player to eliminate.
    If they can't agree, one of the ambassadors is eliminated via rock draw.
  Mechanic: schedulable twist (pre-merge final episode). Each tribe picks ambassador
    based on social + strategic weight. Ambassadors negotiate: if bonds allow agreement
    they pick the highest-threat outsider. If not → rock draw between ambassadors.
  Consequence: one player eliminated without a vote. Massive bond impact on ambassadors.
  VP: dedicated screen with ambassador portraits, negotiation narrative, outcome.

[KL-3] REPLACEMENT (Koh-Lanta medevac rule)
  Trigger: a player is medically evacuated
  What happens: the most recently voted-out player returns to the game.
  Mechanic: on medevac, check gs.eliminated for last voted-out player (not quitters).
    Return them to gs.activePlayers, same tribe. Camp event: shock, bond reset.
  Note: incompatible with RI/Rescue Island (they already handle returnees).

[6] FALSE IDOL PLANT
Trigger: player with strategic >= 8 is actively being targeted AND an idol search is in progress
Roll: ~15%
What happens: player crafts a fake idol and plants it in an obvious location.
  A searching player finds it and believes it's real. If they bring it to tribal and
  play it, it's revealed as fake — humiliating and they lose credibility.
Mechanic: gs.fakeIdols[] entry created. If played at tribal: no votes negated,
  camp event generated next episode, bond collapse.

[11] JURY BITTER CASCADE
Trigger: player on jury with very negative bond (<= -4) toward a finalist
Roll: ~25-40% during FTC phase depending on jury bitterness levels
What happens: the bitter juror's speech is so venomous it poisons other jurors who
  were on the fence. 1-2 nearby jurors shift their vote away from the targeted finalist.
Mechanic: during FTC resolution, re-roll borderline juror votes. Can flip the winner.

[15] FINAL TRIBAL SURPRISE FLIP
Trigger: FTC with jury where one finalist has majority of votes locked in
Roll: ~15% if locked-in finalist has at least one jury member with bond <= 0
What happens: a jury member who seemed locked in has a change of heart based on
  final speeches. One previously-certain vote flips.
Mechanic: final vote resolution re-rolls one borderline juror's vote. Only fires
  when the margin is narrow enough to matter.

[B] NON-MUTUAL RELATIONSHIP (one-sided bond)
Trigger: Player A has bond >= 4 with B but B has low loyalty (<= 3) AND
  B is in a rival alliance that doesn't include A
Roll: passive — detected each episode
What happens: A genuinely trusts B and won't vote them out, but B feels no
  such obligation. Eventually B votes A out and A is blindsided.
Architectural note: recommend perceived bond overlay (gs.perceivedBonds) as first pass.
  Smaller change than full directional bonds.

[KL-2] TIED DESTINIES (Koh-Lanta twist)
  Trigger: post-merge, final 8/10/12. Players randomly paired for the episode.
  What happens: pairs compete together in challenges. At tribal, the person who
    receives the most votes causes their partner's elimination too (double elim).
  Mechanic: schedulable twist. Random pairing at episode start. Challenge scores
    averaged between pairs. Vote targeting considers both members of the pair.
    Two eliminations in one tribal — the voted-out player AND their tied partner.
  Consequence: massive strategic upheaval. Allies paired with enemies. Bond dynamics.
  Note: complex — needs pair-aware voting, double elimination path, VP display.

[KL-4] BLACK VOTE (Koh-Lanta twist)
  Trigger: after being voted out at tribal council
  What happens: the eliminated player casts a secret "Black Vote" against one
    remaining player. This vote counts at the next tribal council the target attends.
  Modern variant (post-Cambodge): instead of an extra vote against someone, the
    eliminated player gives a remaining player an Extra Vote advantage.
  Mechanic: on elimination, eliminated player picks target (highest-heat enemy or
    lowest-bond rival). gs.blackVote = { from, target, ep }. Next tribal: injected
    as extra vote. OR modern: eliminated gives Extra Vote to closest ally.
  Consequence: eliminated players still influence the game. Grudge mechanic.
  Note: two variants (classic = punish, modern = reward). Pick one or make configurable.
