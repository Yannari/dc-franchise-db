Chris: Oh, one more thing! As the winning tribe, Purple sends an emissary to Teal's tribal council tonight. And your emissary is... Fiore! She volunteered. Because of course she did.

Fiore walks into Teal camp. Every head turns.

Fiore: [looking around, calm] Don't mind me. I'm just here to observe.

Wix: [under breath] Great. A spy.

[SCENE: Teal Camp - Fiore's Scouting]

Fiore moves through camp with surgical precision. She talks to everyone. Listens. Watches.

Jake pulls her aside first.

Jake: Look, if you're picking someone to go home tonight -- Eureka. She's a threat and she's unpredictable.

Fiore: [tilts head] Hmm. Maybe.

Wix finds Fiore next.

Wix: Miriam. She's the one nobody's watching, and that's exactly why she's dangerous.

Fiore: [nods slowly] Interesting pitch.

But what Fiore notices most isn't what people say. It's what happens when Miriam walks by. Conversations stop. Eyes shift. People reposition.

[Confessional: Fiore]
Fiore: Jake says Eureka. Wix says Miriam. But the most interesting thing in this camp is what happens when Miriam enters a room. Everybody shuts up. Either she's the most dangerous person here... or she's already lost and nobody's told her yet.

After scouting, Fiore makes her pick.

Fiore: [to Chris, at the emissary station] Wix. Their own tribe wanted them gone. I'm just finishing what they started.

Chris: Brutal. I love it.

The word spreads: Fiore chose Wix. The emissary's pick is locked. Wix is going home -- unless Teal does something about it at tribal.

Thom, Jake, Sterling, Gabby, Dan -- they all register grudges against Fiore for entering their camp and pulling the trigger. Only Dan nods, acknowledging it was a clean read.

[Confessional: Wix]
Wix: Fiore just walked into our camp, talked to five people, and decided I'm the one who goes. [laughs, then stops laughing] My dad always said the truth catches up to you. I just didn't think it'd show up wearing Fiore's face.

[SCENE: Exile Island]

Chris: Oh, and one more twist! Purple, send someone from Teal to Exile Island!

Purple sends Dan.

Dan arrives at Exile alone. Rocky. Sparse. He searches everywhere -- under rocks, inside tree hollows, along the waterline.

Nothing.

Dan: [sitting on a rock, exhausted] Nothing. Empty. [looks at camera] At least I got some peace and quiet.

Chris: [over a speaker hidden in a tree] Dan will return to camp and attend tribal council tonight!

Dan: [startled] Where is that SPEAKER?

[SCENE: Purple Camp - Post-Challenge]

Seraphine finds a forged note -- carefully planted where she'd find it. It's about Lill. Something suggesting Lill is playing both sides.

Seraphine reads it. Pauses. Reads it again.

She's not convinced. But she's not completely dismissive either. The seed is there.

[Confessional: Seraphine]
Seraphine: I found a note about Lill. I don't believe it. [pause] But I didn't throw it away either. And the fact that I didn't throw it away... that worries me more than the note itself.

Fiore finds a different note -- this one planted by Grett. It says Ellie is planning to flip.

Fiore believes every word. The bond between Fiore and Ellie -- already thin -- quietly cracks.

[Confessional: Fiore]
Fiore: Ellie's planning to flip. I saw the note. I believe it because I KNOW her. She's been looking at me differently for days. [cold] That's fine. Now I know where we stand. We don't.

Lill pulls Ellie aside after the fake idol exposure. Says the thing everyone else was thinking but wouldn't say.

Lill: You don't have to prove anything to these people.

Ellie: [rigid] I'm not trying to--

Lill: Yes you are. Every day. Every conversation. You're trying SO hard and it's making it worse. Just... stop. You're already here. That's enough.

Ellie didn't know how badly she needed to hear that. Her shoulders drop half an inch.

[Confessional: Ellie]
Ellie: Lill said I was enough. [long pause] Nobody's said that to me in a really long time.

[SCENE: Purple Camp - Alec and Ellie, Sunrise]

Before anyone else is awake, Alec and Ellie sit at the fire together. They talk in low voices. By the time the rest of the tribe wakes up, the conversation is over. Neither mentions it.

[Confessional: Alec]
Alec: Ellie and I had a conversation this morning. The most important conversation of the episode, probably. [slight smile] Nobody else heard it. That's the point.

[SCENE: Purple Camp - Evening]

Grett goes off. Full breakdown. Loud, uncontrolled, impossible to ignore. She kicks the water bucket. She storms away from the fire. She comes back and kicks the bucket again.

Grett: I'm SICK of this! I'm sick of the food, I'm sick of the bugs, I'm sick of people looking at me like I'm the problem when I'm the only one actually TRYING!

Nick: [frozen] Should we... do something?

Fiore: [not moving] No.

Alec: [also not moving] Definitely not.

Grett storms off into the trees. The tribe exchanges looks.

[Confessional: Nick]
Nick: Grett just... exploded. Like a shaken soda can. I wanted to help but also I wanted to be in a different zip code. Is that bad? [pause] It's probably bad.

[Confessional: Grett]
Grett: I know what that looked like. I don't care. Sometimes you crack because you've been holding everything together for too long. [wipes eyes aggressively] I'm fine. I'm FINE.

[SCENE: Purple Camp - Night]

Nick breaks down in front of Seraphine. Not dramatically. Just honestly. About being here. About feeling like he doesn't measure up.

Nick: I keep thinking everyone's going to realize I don't belong here. Like they made a mistake casting me and any second someone's going to tap me on the shoulder and say "time to go."

Seraphine doesn't try to fix it. Doesn't offer advice. Just sits there.

Nick: [wiping his face] Sorry. I don't know why I--

Seraphine: Don't apologize for being a person, Nick.

They sit in silence for a while. That's enough.

[SCENE: Purple Camp - Day]

Lill carries more than her own body weight from the well without a word. Drops it at camp. Goes back for more.

Alec: [watching] She just... carried all of that.

Seraphine: She does that.

Grett: [from the shelter] She's showing off.

Seraphine: She's being Lill.

Lill gives Ellie permission to stop trying so hard.

Grett talks like she's already won. It registers on every face. She doesn't notice.

[SCENE: Purple Camp]

Seraphine reaches for her usual pre-tribal check-in with Grett. But Grett is already talking to someone else. The moment passes.

Seraphine waits. It keeps passing.

[Confessional: Seraphine]
Seraphine: Grett didn't check in with me today. That's never happened before. [pause] I'm not going to chase her. But I noticed. And the fact that I noticed means it matters more than I want it to.

Grett brings up something Fiore said three days ago -- casually, in front of everyone. The context makes Fiore look bad. Grett acts surprised that it landed that way.

Grett: I'm just saying what she SAID. I didn't put the words in her mouth.

Fiore: [tight-lipped] You know exactly what you're doing.

Grett: [innocent face] I'm just sharing.

[Confessional: Fiore]
Fiore: Grett is passive-aggressive in a way that should be studied by scientists. She drops a bomb, then acts confused by the explosion. [pause] I hate how good she is at it.

[SCENE: Teal Camp - Pre-Tribal Scramble]

The scramble begins the moment they get back from the challenge. Lines are drawn. Then erased. Then redrawn in different colors.

THE FIREWALL huddles. Jake, Sterling, Thom, Dan.

Jake: [spearheading] Wix. Fiore already picked them for the emissary boot. If we pile on, it's clean. Nobody's surprised. Nobody's hurt.

Dan: Makes sense. Wix is a number we can lose.

Thom: [quiet]

Sterling: [nods] Wix it is.

But Thom isn't nodding.

[Confessional: Thom]
Thom: The Firewall says Wix. But Wix is my person. They caught a fish and brought it to ME. They carried wood when their leg was screaming. [exhales] I can't write Wix's name down. I won't. But I can't tell the alliance that without blowing everything up. So I need a different name.

THE CAMP TAKEOVER -- Miriam and Gabby.

Miriam: Sterling. He's tight with Jake, he's tight with Thom, and if we don't split that up now, they'll run the merge.

Gabby: [nods]

But Gabby's mind is elsewhere. Something Miriam said yesterday landed differently today. A promise that felt hollow. A look that didn't match the words.

[Confessional: Gabby]
Gabby: Miriam wants Sterling gone. The Camp Takeover decided. [pause] But the Camp Takeover has a trust problem. I tested Miriam with a piece of information two days ago. A small thing. When it came back wrong... I knew. [jaw sets] The Camp Takeover decided. But I'm deciding something different.

EUREKA AND WIX.

Eureka: Jake. He's the glue. Without Jake, the whole Firewall thing falls apart.

Wix: Jake? You sure?

Eureka: I'm sure.

But Eureka isn't sure. Not about Wix. The limp is gone, sure. But Wix still isn't 100%. And the tribe is watching. And Eureka has been thinking about what's best for the tribe, not just what's best for their duo.

[Confessional: Eureka]
Eureka: I love Wix. I do. But the injury held them back for three challenges, and even though they're moving better... [voice drops] Am I keeping them around because they're my person, or because it's good for the game? [pause] Those aren't the same thing. And I think I just realized that.

Ellie -- visiting from Purple's camp intel -- has been talking to people about Thom and Jake.

Ellie: [to Gabby, quiet] Thom and Jake. Look at them. They're a pair. If you let them get to the merge together, they ride to the final two.

Gabby: [listening]

Ellie: Split them up now. Or watch them win.

[Confessional: Ellie]
Ellie: I can't vote tonight. But I can plant seeds. And the seed I'm planting is simple: Thom and Jake are too close. One of them has to go. [slight smile] I don't care which one.

[SCENE: Teal Camp - Before Tribal]

Jake and Sterling walk to the well together. The conversation from last night -- the real one, about Jake's family -- hangs between them.

Jake: Hey. About what I told you.

Sterling: I didn't tell anyone.

Jake: I know. That's why I'm bringing it up.

Sterling: [cracks knuckles] We're good, man.

But something is off with Jake. He's pulling back from Wix -- not a fight, something quieter. The conversations get shorter. The eye contact disappears.

Wix notices.

Wix: [to Jake] You okay?

Jake: Yeah. Fine.

Wix: You don't seem fine.

Jake: I'm fine, Wix.

[Confessional: Wix]
Wix: Jake and I were tight. Like, genuinely tight. And today... I don't know. Something shifted. He won't look at me. Won't joke around. It's like I got demoted from friend to "person I nod at." [pause] That's how it starts. First they stop looking at you. Then they stop saying your name. Then they write it down.

Thom pushes back on Jake -- hard, publicly.

Thom: You can't just decide for the whole alliance, Jake. That's not how this works.

Jake: I'm not deciding. I'm suggesting.

Thom: You're PUSHING. And you've been pushing all day.

The tribe watches, stunned. Thom and Jake never fight. Ever.

Jake: [stung] Fine. What do you want to do?

Thom: I want everyone to think for themselves for five minutes without you managing the conversation.

Silence.

[Confessional: Jake]
Jake: Thom just called me out in front of everyone. [pause] He's not wrong. I HAVE been pushing. But somebody has to or this tribe eats itself. [runs hands through hair] He's my closest ally and he just made me look like the bad guy. That's... new.

Thom and Sterling share an inside joke before tribal -- a running bit nobody else understands. Every time they make eye contact, one of them breaks into a grin.

Gabby and Sterling run through the numbers quietly.

Gabby: The math is clear.

Sterling: The question is whether anyone else sees it first.

Gabby: [looks at him] They won't. Not if we move now.

Someone lets it slip to Wix that their name is being mentioned. Wix tucks the information away and says nothing.

[Confessional: Wix]
Wix: Someone told me my name's out there. [long pause] My dad would say "just tell them the truth." The truth is that I'm scared. And I'm good at this game. And those two things don't cancel each other out.

[SCENE: Tribal Council - Night]

Torches. The walk in. Eight players sit on stumps. The fire crackles.

Chris: Welcome to Tribal Council. Before we start -- Fiore from Purple is here as emissary. She's already made her pick. But that's later. First, let's talk about your mess.

Chris turns to Wix.

Chris: Wix. You've played this game with your heart. Is that going to be enough tonight?

Wix: [steady] Yeah, I think I might be in trouble. But if people want me out for playing hard, then fine. At least I played.

The bluntness catches people off guard. Gabby's eyebrows go up. Sterling shifts in his seat.

Chris: Dan. If tonight's vote goes sideways -- and it might -- how much of that lands on you?

Dan: [measured] Plans change. What matters is whether you've done the work before tonight. I have.

Chris: [smiling] Translation: you're the chess player.

Dan: I'm a player. Period.

Chris focuses on Eureka.

Chris: Eureka. You've been described as unpredictable this week. Is that fair?

Eureka: There's been a lot of noise this week. I'm just trying to make the vote I can live with.

Both sides think they have Eureka. One of them is wrong.

Chris: Every tribal has one moment where the real game shows up. I feel like we're about to have that moment. Wix?

Wix: The people I trusted made a decision without me. I hope it works out for them.

Dan: I don't have anything to say to that. Tonight will speak for itself.

The confrontation is out. The tribe absorbs it. Some shift. Some go very still.

Chris looks between Thom and Jake.

Chris: You two have been close out here. Does that protect you both -- or make you both a target?

Thom: I trust Jake completely. And I think that trust is real -- on both sides.

Jake: Thom and I are close. That's not going to change.

Chris: [leaning back] Famous last words. It is time to vote.

[SCENE: Voting]

The players file to the voting booth one by one.

Dan votes. We see the parchment: WIX. Dan holds it up.

Dan: The Firewall agreed on this name. I'm going along with it.

Miriam votes. STERLING. She folds it deliberately.

Miriam: This vote makes sense. I'm staying with the plan.

Thom votes. We see: GABBY.

[Confessional: Thom]
Thom: [at voting booth, holding up the parchment] Wix is my person. I can't do it. Gabby pays for it.

Jake votes. We don't see the name.

[Confessional: Jake]
Jake: [at voting booth, staring at the parchment] The target was Wix. But I can't pull the trigger. [writes a different name] I'm sorry.

Eureka votes. We see: WIX.

[Confessional: Eureka]
Eureka: [at voting booth, voice cracking slightly] Two targets tonight. The one I told everyone about... and this one. The injury held Wix back. Voting them out is doing them a favor. And the tribe a service. [folds parchment] That doesn't make it feel better.

Sterling votes. Gabby votes. Wix votes.

[SCENE: Vote Reading]

Chris: I'll go tally the votes.

He returns with the urn.

Chris: If anybody has a hidden immunity idol and would like to play it, now would be the time to do so.

Silence. Nobody moves. Gabby's hand doesn't twitch. Miriam sits still.

Chris: Once the votes are read, the decision is final. Person voted out will be asked to leave the tribal council area immediately. I'll read the votes.

Chris: First vote... Gabby.

Gabby blinks.

Chris: Eureka.

Eureka's eyes widen.

Chris: Wix.

Wix nods, barely.

Chris: Miriam.

Miriam's jaw tightens.

Chris: Sterling.

Sterling looks around the tribe, confused.

Chris: Wix. That's two votes Wix, one Gabby, one Eureka, one Miriam, one Sterling.

Chris: Miriam. Two votes Wix, two votes Miriam.

Chris: Jake.

Jake's head snaps up. He stares at Sterling. Sterling looks away.

Chris: [holding the last parchment] That's two votes Wix, two votes Miriam, one Gabby, one Eureka, one Sterling, one Jake. We are DEADLOCKED. No majority.

The tribe erupts in whispers.

Chris: Here's what happens. Wix and Miriam -- you cannot vote. Everyone else will revote. You can ONLY vote for Wix or Miriam. If we deadlock again... we go to rocks.

[SCENE: Revote]

The revote happens fast. Tense. Frantic whispers before they go up.

Thom votes: WIX. He flipped to avoid rocks.

[Confessional: Thom]
Thom: [at voting booth, agonized] I chose numbers over loyalty. I hate myself for it.

Eureka votes: WIX. Held position.

Jake votes: MIRIAM. Flipped to avoid rocks.

Sterling votes: MIRIAM. Flipped to avoid rocks.

Gabby votes: WIX. Flipped to avoid rocks.

Dan votes: MIRIAM. Flipped to avoid rocks.

[SCENE: Revote Reading]

Chris: I'll read the revote. Remember -- Wix or Miriam only.

Chris: Wix.

Chris: Miriam.

Chris: Wix.

Chris: Miriam.

Chris: Miriam. That's three votes Miriam, two votes Wix, one vote left.

Chris holds the final parchment. Opens it slowly.

Chris: ...Wix.

Three-three. Deadlocked again.

Chris: [grinning] We are deadlocked AGAIN. Which means... it's time for rocks.

The color drains from every face.

Chris: Wix and Miriam -- you're both safe. You will not draw rocks. Everyone else? One of you is going home tonight, and it won't be because of how you played. It'll be because of luck.

Chris produces a bag with colored rocks. One purple rock among white ones. Draw the purple rock, you're out.

Thom reaches in. White.

Jake reaches in. White.

Sterling reaches in. White.

Gabby reaches in. White.

Dan reaches in. White.

Eureka reaches in. She holds the rock in her closed fist. Looks at the sky. Opens her hand.

Purple.

Silence.

Total, absolute silence.

[Confessional: Eureka]
Eureka: [filmed later, tear streaks on her face but composed] I talked to everybody. I listened to everybody. I made everyone feel heard. And they still chose to write my name down -- wait. They DIDN'T write my name down. Not enough of them, anyway. A ROCK sent me home. A random, meaningless, stupid little rock. [voice breaks, then steadies] Maybe being liked isn't enough. Maybe you have to be feared too.

Chris: Eureka. The tribe has spoken.

Eureka stands. She doesn't look at anyone. She doesn't say goodbye. She walks to the torch stand.

Chris snuffs her torch.

Then she stops. Turns around. Looks at Wix.

Eureka: Play hard. For both of us.

Wix: [barely holding it together] I will.

Eureka walks the path out. Her torch smoke hangs in the air long after she's gone.

The tribe sits in silence. Nobody chose to send Eureka home. A rock did. And nobody knows how to talk about it.

Chris: Oh, one more thing. Fiore?

Fiore stands. She's been watching from the emissary bench the entire time.

Fiore: [to the tribe] Wix. Your own tribe wanted you gone. I'm just making it official.

Wix closes their eyes.

Chris: Wix, the emissary has spoken. Your torch, please.

Wix stands. Walks to Chris. Hands over the torch.

Chris snuffs it.

Wix: [to the tribe, quiet] The people I trusted made a decision without me. I just hope it was worth it.

Nobody answers.

[Confessional: Wix]
Wix: [filmed at exit] I lied about a lot of things out here. Who I was, where I'm from, what I've done. But the friendships? Those were real. Thom was real. The fish was real. [laughs, then stops] My dad always said I'd be fine if I just told the truth. Turns out, the truth wasn't enough either. But at least I know now.

[SCENE: Teal Camp - Night, Post-Tribal]

The six remaining Teal members walk back to camp. Nobody speaks for a long time.

Thom sits at the fire. Stares at where Wix used to sit. Where Eureka used to sit.

Jake sits across from him. Neither speaks.

Miriam walks to the shelter alone. She survived by a single vote. She doesn't scramble. She doesn't celebrate. She lies down and closes her eyes.

Gabby checks her bag. Two advantages. Still secret. Still loaded.

[Confessional: Thom]
Thom: Two people gone tonight. One by bad luck. One by an emissary who spent twenty minutes in our camp. [long pause] I voted for Gabby in the first round and Wix in the revote. I betrayed my person to save myself from a rock. [voice drops] I don't know if I can live with that. I'm gonna have to.

[Confessional: Dan]
Dan: [calm] The Firewall's plan was Wix. Instead, we got a four-way split, a tie, a revote, another tie, and a rock draw that took out the one person nobody was targeting. [slight smile] The game did what the game does. Chaos won tonight. Tomorrow I make sure it doesn't win again.

Chris: [standing at the tribal council set, alone, torch-lit behind him] Nobody planned this. Nobody wanted this. And NOBODY knows what happens next. The merge is coming. The numbers are scrambled. And somewhere in this camp, there's a hidden idol nobody's found yet. Who survives? Who cracks? And can Miriam dig herself out of the grave she almost fell into?
