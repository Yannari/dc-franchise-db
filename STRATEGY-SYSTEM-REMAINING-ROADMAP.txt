1. FULL-SEASON INTEGRATION AUDIT  ✅ BUILT (tests/full-season-audit.test.js — see Progress above)

Run many complete seasons instead of isolated tribals and measure:
- Average alliance lifespan
- Alliances formed, dissolved, and re-formed per season
- Average betrayals, exposed pitches, apologies, truces, and expulsions
- Repeat targeting and recurring rivalries
- Idol play accuracy, wasted idols, ally plays, and missed danger
- Reputation labels earned per contestant and per season
- Whether reputations change naturally instead of becoming permanent too early
- Jury outcomes and whether reputation has a reasonable influence
- Frequency of unanimous, majority, plurality, tied, and rock-draw votes
- Whether different archetypes and stat profiles produce visibly different games

Suggested sample size:
- 100–500 complete seasons for automated metrics
- 5–10 manually reviewed seasons for narrative continuity


2. CROSS-SCREEN STORY CONTINUITY REVIEW

For selected episodes, compare this exact sequence:

Camp events
→ Voting Plans
→ Tribal Council ballots
→ Why This Vote Happened
→ Text backlog / debug
→ Next episode’s alliance fallout

Check that:
- Camp hints do not reveal exact ballots prematurely
- Voting Plans accurately describe known numbers and uncertainty
- Late changes have an understandable trigger
- Ballot reasons match the actual plan, bond, memory, or counterplay
- Idol decisions match the holder’s perceived information
- Alliance fallout references the correct plan and betrayal
- Repair scenes occur only when the betrayal was detected
- Reputation changes are supported by repeated visible behavior
- The eliminated-player explanation matches the votes that counted


3. REPETITION AND WRITING CLEANUP

Search for overused wording and events:
- “This vote makes sense”
- “The plan still needs people to hold”
- “No credible late coalition”
- “The heat has been building”
- “This isn’t strategy — it’s honesty”
- Repeated pitch, goat, betrayal, and reputation cards

Add more contextual variants, but do not sacrifice accuracy.
Prefer one consolidated event over several cards describing the same development.

  high-loyalty commitments, and temporary strategic overlap.
- Added four equally precise Held Commitment explanations.
- Added first-person conversions so the variants remain natural in confessionals.
- Prose-only selection uses a stable hash and consumes no gameplay RNG.
- Added tests/strategy-writing-variety.test.js to prevent the former stock lines
  from dominating season-long ballot explanations.

7. FINAL BALANCING

Only tune after the full-season audit.

Watch for:
- Persuasive reputation snowballing into automatic pitch success
- Unreliable or Leaky labels making a player permanently unusable
- Alliances forgiving too easily or never forgiving
- Strategy exclusion lasting too long
- Counterplay reversing too many votes
- Idol holders reading danger too accurately
- Excessive plurality chaos
- Too many unanimous votes
- Reputations appearing after too little evidence

Re-run the realism audit after every meaningful probability change.
