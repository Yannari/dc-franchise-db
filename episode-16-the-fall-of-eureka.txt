TOTAL DRAMA: CURSED ISLAND
EPISODE 16 — "The Fall of Eureka"
Post-Merge | Final Six → Final Four

================================================================
COLD OPEN
================================================================

[Black screen. Chris McLean's voice, delighted.]

Chris (V.O.): Previously on Total Drama: Cursed Island!

[Quick cuts: the merged camp at dusk. Six players around a struggling fire. Thom and Alec on opposite logs, not looking at each other. Fiore standing slightly apart, watching everyone.]

Chris (V.O.): Six left. And somehow the two people who hate each other most are still both breathing. Thom won't even say Alec's name out loud anymore. Calls him "that person." Cute.

[Cut: Fiore, mid-confessional from an earlier day, calm.]

Chris (V.O.): Fiore decided she's running the game. Said it out loud. To everyone. Bold. We'll see how that ages.

[Cut: Eureka laughing with Gabby. Then Eureka leaning in close to Alec, saying something private.]

Chris (V.O.): And Eureka's been everybody's best friend. Which is a great way to make it far... and a great way to make people nervous.

[Cut: the empty beach. Six torches.]

Chris (V.O.): Tonight, I hand them a twist that ties their fates together — literally. Somebody's going home. Maybe two somebodies. Find out right now, on Total! Drama! CURSED ISLAND!

[THEME SONG.]

================================================================
ACT ONE — CAMP, MORNING
================================================================

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Fire Pit, early. The fire is low. All six are present: Thom feeding kindling, Miriam beside him, Eureka and Gabby sharing the good log, Alec on the far end, Fiore standing.]

[Chris walks into camp uninvited, holding a coil of handcuffs over one shoulder.]

Chris: Morning, final six. Quick announcement before you get too comfortable.

Alec: You brought jewelry.

Chris: I brought destiny. New twist. It's called Tied Destinies. You've been randomly paired. Today's immunity challenge — you run it cuffed to your partner. And here's the fun part: at Tribal, if your partner goes home...

[He lets it hang.]

Chris: ...so do you.

[Silence. Then everyone talking at once.]

Chris: Pairs! Thom and Alec.

Thom: [flat] No.

Alec: [to the sky] Of course.

Chris: Miriam and Fiore. And — Eureka and Gabby.

[Eureka and Gabby look at each other. Gabby exhales.]

Chris: Challenge is in an hour. Try not to murder each other before then. Or do. Ratings.

[He leaves.]

[Confessional: Gabby]

Gabby: Out of every name in that hat, I get Eureka. The one person I'd actually pick. [knocks on the log] I'm not saying it out loud again. But yeah. We got lucky.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — the moment the strategizing starts.]

[The second Chris is gone, camp fractures into whispers. Eureka pulls Gabby a few feet away.]

Eureka: Okay. We're tied. So we don't just protect ourselves anymore — we protect us. If somebody comes for me, they get you too. That's leverage.

Gabby: That's a target.

Eureka: It's both. We make sure nobody wants the math.

[Confessional: Eureka]

Eureka: Every conversation today is going to circle back to the same question. Which pair do we throw away? [pause] My whole game has been making people like me. Today I find out if "liked" is worth anything when the knives come out.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Treeline.]

[Fiore catches Gabby alone. Quick, low.]

Fiore: If you and Eureka are smart, you point this whole thing at Thom and Miriam. Two threats, one vote. We'll never get a cleaner shot.

Gabby: [noncommittal] Mm.

[Fiore moves off. Gabby watches her go.]

[Confessional: Gabby]

Gabby: Fiore floated a plan and it felt rushed. Too clean, too fast, like she'd rehearsed it on the walk over. [pause] I don't know what she's holding. But she's holding something. I can read that much.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — by the water.]

[Fiore, to the group, unprompted, loud enough to carry:]

Fiore: I'll save everyone the guessing. I'm running this game. Everyone here knows it. So let's stop pretending the conversations matter.

[The camp goes quiet. Nobody argues. Nobody agrees either.]

[Confessional: Alec]

Alec: Fiore just announced she's in charge. To a tribe that's looking for exactly one reason to vote somebody out. [small smile] I didn't have to plant that. She planted it on herself.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Shelter.]

[Alec sits down next to Miriam, casual.]

Alec: You want to hear something about Eureka? Because there's a version of her you're not seeing.

Miriam: [not looking up] Go ahead.

Alec: She's been telling two different rooms two different votes. You included.

[Miriam listens. Says nothing. Lets him finish.]

[Confessional: Miriam]

Miriam: Alec came to me with a story about Eureka. Wrapped nice. Delivered nicer. [pause] And that's exactly why I didn't buy it. Real information doesn't come gift-wrapped. I'm not filing Eureka's name away. I'm filing his.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Fire Pit.]

[But the next one lands. Fiore finds Miriam and tells her something — quietly, plainly, no flourish. Miriam's face changes.]

Miriam: ...she said that? About me?

Fiore: I wouldn't repeat it if it didn't matter.

Miriam: [quiet] Thank you. For telling me.

[Fiore nods and walks off.]

[Confessional: Fiore]

Fiore: The trick isn't lying. Anybody can lie. The trick is telling it so flat, so bored, that they thank you for the knife. [pause] Eureka has no idea her standing just dropped. Good.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Treeline, later.]

[Miriam walks up to Eureka. The temperature shifts. A few heads turn.]

Miriam: Did you say my game was a problem? To Fiore?

Eureka: What? No. Miriam, no —

Miriam: Because that's what's going around.

Eureka: Then it's going around from someone who wants us fighting. Think about who that helps.

[Miriam holds her stare a beat too long. Camp has gone very quiet.]

[Confessional: Eureka]

Eureka: Somebody got to Miriam. And I can guess who. [exhales] The problem with being everyone's friend is everyone can use your name. I've spent fifteen episodes being safe. Today it feels like a costume that doesn't fit anymore.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Night before the challenge.]

[The fight burns off. Thom sits with Eureka long after the others have gone quiet, just keeping her company. He doesn't fix it. He doesn't strategize. He's just there.]

Thom: You don't have to talk.

Eureka: I know.

[They sit. The fire pops.]

[Confessional: Thom]

Thom: Eureka's the only person in this game I trust completely. [pause] That's either going to save me or get me killed. I'm okay with both.

================================================================
ACT TWO — IMMUNITY CHALLENGE: TRIAL BY TRI-ARMED TRIATHLON
================================================================

[SCENE: Challenge Beach. Three stations stretch down the sand: a row of covered platters, three canoes at the shore, and a pile of carved wooden heads. Chris waits, cuffs in hand.]

Chris: Welcome to the Trial by Tri-Armed Triathlon! It sounds cooler than it is. It's about as cool as it sounds. Three events — Chowdown, Idol Haul, Totem Pole. First pair to take two events wins invincibility. And you're running it like this.

[Chef moves down the line, clicking cuffs onto each pair.]

Chris: Anybody want to wimp out? There's a key. Take it, you're free — and out of the running.

[Nobody moves.]

Chris: No takers. Brave. Stupid. My favorite combo. Event one — Chowdown!

[Chef whips the cloches off the platters. Mystery meat. A brown sauce with no name. Something that was once green chicken. The green chicken twitches.]

Chris: One of you feeds, one of you eats. Clear the platter first, win the round. GO!

[SCENE: Challenge — Chowdown.]

[Alec grabs the fork immediately, points it at Thom.]

Alec: You're eating.

Thom: [sitting, opening his mouth] Don't enjoy it.

Alec: No promises.

[The green chicken moves on the fork. Thom gags. Alec freezes, genuinely unsure what to do with a moving entrée.]

[Across the sand, Fiore's arm pulls tight against the cuff — she can't reach the center of Miriam's platter.]

Fiore: I can't — the chain —

Miriam: [leaning in to meet her] Then I'll come to you. Aim.

[Eureka and Gabby have a three-second argument over who eats. Eureka wins it, takes the spoon.]

Gabby: You sure?

Eureka: Don't look at it. Look at me.

[They find a rhythm fast. Scoop. Swallow. Scoop. Swallow. Almost choreographed.]

[Confessional: Eureka]

Eureka: The food was a war crime. But Gabby just locked in. No complaining, no drama. [pause] That's the kind of person you want on the other end of a handcuff. And, honestly, the other end of everything.

[Desperate, Alec abandons the fork entirely, picks up the whole platter, and smashes it into Thom's face.]

Chris: That — somehow — counts!

[But it's not enough. Eureka and Gabby's platter is already empty.]

Chris: Eureka and Gabby take Chowdown! One-nothing!

[Thom wipes brown sauce off his chin and says nothing. Alec looks weirdly proud.]

[SCENE: Challenge — Event Two: Idol Haul.]

Chris: Paddle to Boney Island, grab the cursed idol, get it to the Cave of Treacherous Terror. First pair there wins. GO!

[Three canoes hit the water. Thom and Alec immediately argue about who's paddling harder. Neither is, really. Then, mid-lake, something shifts.]

Alec: [not looking at him] For the record. Week two. The thing with the rice. That was me.

Thom: [a long pause, paddling] ...I know.

[They paddle in silence for a minute. It's not a comfortable silence. But it's a different one.]

[On Boney Island, Alec lifts the wrapped idol — and his knee buckles.]

Alec: The curse is — okay, the curse is real, I can't —

Thom: [crouching, back turned] Get on.

Alec: You're joking.

Thom: I want to win more than I want to watch you crawl. Get on.

[Alec climbs onto Thom's back. Thom starts up the trail.]

Alec: [from up top, quiet] I know we haven't talked much. Thanks for this.

[Thom grunts. Genuinely moved. Hates that he is.]

[Confessional: Thom]

Thom: I carried the person I hate most in this game up a hill on my back. [pause] Don't read into it. There was a million dollars at the top.

[Miriam and Fiore are ahead early — Fiore spots the island through the mist, Miriam corrects course — but their piggyback collapses halfway up the trail and Fiore lands badly. Wooly beavers surge from the cave mouth and scatter them.]

Fiore: [scrambling backward] WHAT is a wooly beaver —

Miriam: NOT THE TIME.

[Thom hauls Alec to the cave and they drop the idol in.]

Chris: Thom and Alec take Idol Haul! We're tied — one and one. Winner of the Totem decides it all!

[SCENE: Challenge — Event Three: Totem Pole.]

[Chris uncovers a pile of carved wooden heads — one for every eliminated player.]

Chris: Stack them in elimination order. First boot on the bottom. GO!

[Thom picks up a head and stops cold.]

Thom: [quiet] Drew. They deserved better.

[He places it carefully. Then, two heads later, he stalls — confused, frustrated, the cuff yanking him off balance.]

Thom: I'm — I'm done, I am DONE —

Alec: [steadying the chain] You're not. Pass me that one. Work with me. Just this once.

[They find a grip. Shoulder to shoulder, passing heads down the line.]

[At the next station over, Fiore holds up a head and smirks.]

Fiore: Oh, Will. Zero game, zero threat. Barely a player.

Miriam: [snatching the next one] Hey. Dan was funny. Don't talk about them like that.

[Eureka and Gabby get tangled on the order.]

Eureka: Is this Sterling or Seraphine?

Gabby: Does it matter?

Eureka: It matters a LOT right now.

[The chain stretches; four-foot cuffs, six-foot totem. Everyone loses time to the same problem.]

[Thom and Alec lock in the last head.]

Chris: THAT'S IT! Thom and Alec complete the Totem — and WIN invincibility!

[The two of them stand there, breathing hard, still cuffed, having just won the most important challenge of the season by accident and spite.]

Alec: [offering his free hand] Good game. I mean it.

Thom: [looks at the hand] ...don't.

[He turns and walks, dragging Alec a step behind him. Chef cuts the winning pair loose.]

[Confessional: Alec]

Alec: We hate each other. We are immune. Together. [pause] The two ends of every argument in this game just became untouchable. Which means tonight, the middle gets eaten alive.

================================================================
ACT THREE — POST-CHALLENGE SCRAMBLE
================================================================

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Afternoon. Thunder rolls in the distance and never arrives. The whole tribe stays on edge waiting for a storm that won't come.]

[Thom, gathering firewood, starts humming something under his breath without thinking. By the time the fire's going, half the camp is humming it too, nobody sure where it started.]

[Confessional: Miriam]

Miriam: Thom and I won nothing today — except we didn't have to. [pause] I didn't talk much at camp. I didn't need to. The pieces are already where I put them. The only question is whether Fiore knows she's standing on one.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Shelter. The Tribal Terrors — Thom and Miriam — talk low.]

Thom: Fiore. She's the player. She's the one you can't beat at the end.

Miriam: Agreed. But if we send Fiore, Miriam-and-Fiore are tied. I go too.

[Beat. They both sit with it.]

Thom: ...then it can't be your pair pulling the trigger. It has to look like it came from somewhere else.

Miriam: Eureka.

Thom: Eureka.

[Confessional: Thom]

Thom: "I trust you more than anyone out here." I said it first. Miriam didn't hesitate. [pause] The Tribal Terrors are real. The problem with this twist is that loving someone in this game is now a way to lose twice.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Treeline. Alec works Eureka, smooth and patient.]

Alec: You want Fiore gone. I want Gabby gone. Funny how those don't conflict.

Eureka: They conflict a lot, actually. Gabby's my partner. She goes, I go.

Alec: [stepping closer] Which is why you make very sure tonight is about Fiore. Push it. You've got the room. People listen to you.

[Confessional: Alec]

Alec: I've been running three conversations through Eureka all day, and she thinks she's the one talking. [pause] I tell her to push the Fiore vote. Loudly. Because the louder Eureka pushes, the more Fiore looks at Eureka. And the more Fiore looks at Eureka — the safer Gabby's enemies get.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — by the water.]

[Gabby catches Eureka's name floating in a conversation she wasn't in. She kills it on the spot.]

Gabby: Not Eureka.

[That's all. Two words. The conversation moves on.]

[Confessional: Gabby]

Gabby: Somebody floated Eureka's name and I shut it down before I even decided to. [pause] It's not strategy at this point. It's reflex. Which probably means it's the thing that gets me in trouble. I'm okay with that.

================================================================
ACT FOUR — TRIBAL COUNCIL
================================================================

[SCENE: Tribal Council — Night. Six players, six torches. Thom and Alec wear immunity like a wall between everyone else.]

Chris: [settling on Fiore] You've been in the middle of every conversation this week. Does that help you tonight, or hurt you?

Fiore: I feel exactly where I need to be. I've played clean, kept my commitments, and I'm ready for whatever comes.

[Chris lets the composure sit there. It either reassures her allies or sets off every alarm in the people who didn't plan with her.]

Chris: Eureka. You've been one of the loudest voices about where this vote goes. Is tonight going the way you planned?

Eureka: Look — I pushed for this vote. I think it's the move. If I'm wrong, I'll own it.

[Across the fire, Fiore's eyes settle on Eureka and stay there. Eureka just told her target exactly who pulled the trigger.]

Chris: Miriam. Does tonight feel like a game move, or does it feel personal?

Miriam: Game. Always. I keep the emotion out of it. Tonight's no different.

[A little too clean. Somebody here doesn't buy it.]

Chris: Fiore, Eureka — there's tension there. Are you two actually okay?

Fiore: I'll be direct. Eureka's been running this vote. I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

Eureka: I think Fiore's a smart player. That's exactly why we're having this conversation.

[The confrontation is out in the open now. Some of the tribe shifts. Some go very still. The air has changed.]

Chris: It's time to vote. Thom, you're up.

[VOTING — quick cuts.]

[Thom, writing FIORE.]
Thom: Trust is everything out here. I'm following my people.

[Miriam, writing GABBY.]
Miriam: Nothing personal. The plan's the plan.

[Gabby, writing MIRIAM.]
Gabby: This one's personal. It's been personal since before we ever got here.

[Alec, writing GABBY.]
Alec: I told you weeks ago I'd make sure you didn't see the end. I keep my promises. The bad ones especially.

[Fiore, writing EUREKA.]
Fiore: Something about you points the wrong direction. I'm not waiting to find out where.

[Eureka, writing FIORE.]
Eureka: Loyal to the plan. I don't break ranks the one night it counts.

[SCENE: Tribal Council — Vote Reading.]

Chris: If anybody's got an advantage they want to play, now's the time.

[Nobody moves. Yet.]

Chris: I'll read the votes. First vote... Fiore.

[Fiore, still.]

Chris: Gabby. Gabby. That's two Gabby, one Fiore.

[Gabby's jaw sets.]

Chris: Miriam. ...Fiore. We're tied. Two Fiore, two Gabby, one Miriam. One vote left.

[Long pause.]

Chris: Last vote... Fiore.

[Fiore is on the chopping block. And she's already standing.]

Fiore: Chris. I've got something.

[She reaches into her bag and pulls out a small, ugly amulet.]

Chris: [grinning] Well. The Second Life Amulet.

Fiore: I'm playing it. For me.

Chris: Then you don't go home tonight — but you don't walk free either. The Amulet sends you to a duel. Pick who you face. Loser goes.

[Fiore looks slowly around the fire. Lands on Eureka.]

Fiore: Eureka.

[Eureka's face doesn't fall. It just... settles. Like she already knew.]

[Confessional: Eureka]

Eureka: She picked me. Out of everyone, she picked me. [small, tired laugh] Of course she did. I'm the one she's most afraid of and the one she's most sure she can beat. That's the whole story of my game in one sentence.

[SCENE: Tribal Council — The Duel: Speed Puzzle.]

Chris: Two frames. Identical puzzles. First to finish stays. Survivors, you ready? GO.

[Fiore and Eureka tear into the pieces. It's fast, ugly, close. Eureka gets ahead early — then stalls on the last corner. Fiore's hands don't stop. She slots the final piece.]

Chris: FIORE finishes! Fiore stays.

[Eureka sets down the piece in her hand. Gently. Like she doesn't want to break it.]

Chris: Eureka — the tribe, and the curse, have spoken. Bring me your torch.

[She does. But before Chris can lift the snuffer, Gabby is on her feet.]

Gabby: Wait. Wait — we're TIED. Tied Destinies. If she goes —

Chris: [almost kind] That's right, Gabby.

[He snuffs Eureka's torch.]

Chris: Eureka's fire is out.

[And without Gabby moving an inch —]

Chris: ...so is yours.

[Gabby's torch goes dark. She stares at it. Then crosses to Eureka and pulls her into a hug.]

Gabby: We were supposed to —

Eureka: I know. I know.

[They walk to the Dock of Shame together. Neither looks back.]

[Confessional: Eureka — Final Words]

Eureka: I kept my word every single day. Every vote, every conversation — I was honest. And in this game, that's apparently a weakness. [pause] Loyalty gets you a seat on the jury. Not a seat at the end. Everybody liked me. Turns out that's just the safest name to write down.

[Confessional: Gabby — Final Words]

Gabby: Eureka took me out the door with her. [pause] I'm not mad at her. I'd never be mad at her. I'm mad at the twist, and I'm mad at the people who used it. That's a different fire, and it doesn't go out when you snuff a torch.

[SCENE: Tribal Council — Aftermath. Four remain. Thom. Miriam. Alec. Fiore. Two pairs who can't stand each other, one challenge from the end.]

Chris: And then there were four. The loyal ones. The hateful ones. And nobody left in the middle to hide behind. [grins] This is going to be fun. Get some sleep.

[SCENE: Merged Camp — Night.]

[Fiore sits by the fire alone. Miriam lowers herself onto the log beside her, the one person who, by the twist, lives and dies with her now.]

Miriam: You picked Eureka.

Fiore: I picked the one I could beat.

Miriam: And tomorrow?

Fiore: Tomorrow we're still tied to each other. So I guess we'd better hope we're both very good at this.

[They watch the fire. Neither moves.]

Chris (V.O.): Four left. Two duos who'd rather lose than help each other. Who wins the final immunity? Who's making fire? And who walks away with a million dollars? Find out next time — on Total! Drama! CURSED ISLAND!

TOTAL DRAMA AFTERMATH #4 — "Receipts"
================================================================

[SCENE: The Aftermath Studio. Bright lights, a curved couch, a restless crowd. Chris struts out in a blazer he did not earn.]

Chris: Welcome back to the Aftermath! I'm your host, Chris McLean, and tonight the studio is STACKED with people the island spit out. Give it up for our guests — Eureka! Ellie! Gabby! And Jake!

[The four walk out to applause. In the bleachers behind them, the Peanut Gallery — the season's earlier casualties — heckle and cheer: Wix, Will, Drew, Ashley, Grett, Sterling, Nick, Lill, Dan, Seraphine.]

Chris: And a special note — Eureka, you are the only player this season to get voted out TWICE. Once back in episode five. Then you clawed your way back in... and the island got you again in episode sixteen.

Eureka: [laughing] When you put it like that it sounds like a personal grudge.

Chris: The island holds grudges. So do I. Sit down.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 1 — THE HOT SEAT
----------------------------------------------------------------

Chris: Eureka. You're first. You came back from the dead and made it all the way to the final six. Who do you blame for the second goodbye?

Eureka: Honestly? The twist. Tied Destinies handed Fiore a loophole and she used it perfectly. I don't even blame her for that — I'd have done the same. I blame the math.

Chris: Biggest mistake?

Eureka: I waited. Every season they tell you "don't wait" and every season somebody waits. I was so busy being everybody's friend I forgot to be somebody's nightmare.

Chris: Who wins?

Eureka: Miriam. If Thom doesn't drag her down with him, she's holding that check.

Chris: Last words?

Eureka: It was an experience. I came back for more of it. No regrets — except the obvious one.

[Crowd: wild.]

Chris: Ellie! Out in episode thirteen. Crowd, she did NOT see it coming.

Ellie: [still wincing] I really didn't. I'm still processing it, if I'm honest.

Chris: Blame?

Ellie: Me. I trusted a read I should've double-checked. You can't un-ring that bell.

Chris: Prediction?

Ellie: Fiore. People keep saying she's too obvious to win. People said that about a lot of winners.

Chris: Last words?

Ellie: I gave it everything I had. I just gave it to the wrong person.

[Crowd: wild.]

Chris: Gabby! Fresh off the boat — you just got home this episode, collateral on Eureka's torch. How's that feel?

Gabby: Like getting a ticket for somebody else's parking job. [shrugs] But if I'm going down with anyone, it's her. I meant it on the island and I mean it here.

Chris: Blame?

Gabby: The people who knew the twist would take us both and pointed it at Eureka anyway. They know who they are.

Chris: Who wins?

Gabby: Not Fiore, if there's any justice. Which there isn't. So... Fiore.

Chris: Last words?

Gabby: I gave it everything I had. And I'd do the loyalty part exactly the same. That's the part I'm keeping.

[Crowd: wild.]

Chris: And Jake! Booted episode fourteen. You told us on the way out you'd be back.

Jake: And I will be. Count on it.

Chris: Blame?

Jake: Nobody. I played a double game, it caught up with me, that's the cost of doing business.

Chris: Prediction?

Jake: Alec. Everybody's so busy hating him they forgot to vote him out.

Chris: Last words?

Jake: I played my game. I'm proud of what I did out there. See you next season — count on it.

[Crowd: wild.]

----------------------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 2 — TRUTH OR ANVIL
----------------------------------------------------------------

Chris: You know the rules. I read the receipts. You tell the truth — or an anvil tells it for you. Eureka. Front and center.

[Eureka steps onto the marked square. A shadow falls over it.]

Chris: You were in The Outwit Club. You were ALSO in The Collective. Two alliances, two different targets, and you told each room you were loyal. Truth? Or do I drop the tape?

Eureka: [exhales] You got me. I owned it then, I'll own it now.

Chris: TRUTH! No anvil. [to camera] And note, folks — Thom has NO idea his ride-or-die just admitted that. But this airs. And when it does, that changes everything.

[The Peanut Gallery murmurs.]

Chris: Ellie. Square.

Chris: You and Fiore — civil, polite, never a public fight. But the numbers say you hated her quietly the whole time.

Ellie: I'm not going to sit here and lie about it. I did what I did.

Chris: TRUTH! Fiore's going to hear about this. Next episode. You're welcome.

Chris: Gabby.

Chris: You made a final-two deal with Fiore in episode fifteen. You shook on it. Looked her dead in the eye.

Gabby: ...yeah. I mean — yeah. You already know the answer.

Chris: TRUTH! And here's the kicker — Fiore made that same deal meaning none of it. You two deserved each other.

Chris: Jake. Last one.

Chris: You were in The Firewall. You were ALSO in The Bridge. Different targets. You swore loyalty to both.

Jake: I'm not going to lie to you. Not here. Not now.

Chris: TRUTH across the board! Nobody took the anvil! Worst Aftermath ever — these people are too HONEST in the off-season.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 3 — UNSEEN FOOTAGE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Chris: Roll the tape the editors buried.

[Screen — EPISODE 15.]
Chris (V.O.): Fiore shaking Gabby's hand on that final-two deal. Big smile. Firm grip. She never meant a single word of it.

[Gabby, in the studio, slow-claps once.]

[Screen — EPISODE 13.]
Chris (V.O.): And here's Gabby planting false information with Alec. It stayed quiet. Alec passed the test without ever knowing it was a test — and earned Gabby's trust without earning anything at all.

Gabby: [to the crowd] See? I make moves. Nobody ever clips them.

----------------------------------------------------------------
SEGMENT 4 — FAN CALL
----------------------------------------------------------------

Chris: Time for our fan segment. We've got a caller live on the line. Go ahead — you're on with Eureka.

[A screen lights up: PAT — "THE HATER" — LIVE.]

Pat: Your bond with Fiore was negative five-point-two. One of the lowest in the entire game. What did she do to you?

Eureka: We just clashed. Different play styles, different values. I tried to make it work, and it didn't.

Chris: Wow. The haters do NOT hold back.

Pat: You played for five episodes without making a single big move. Was that the plan, or did you just... not?

Eureka: [pause] ...I know. I waited too long. That's the lesson.

Chris: Easy there, Pat — that's our guest.

Pat: You were in two alliances. The Outwit Club, The Collective. Which one was real?

Eureka: At the time, all of them felt real. Looking back... probably none of them.

Chris: Thanks for calling in, Pat. That's our fan segment for tonight — and I think Eureka needs a minute.

[Eureka, half-smiling, shakes her head.]

----------------------------------------------------------------

Chris: That's our show! Four players home, four still fighting, and a final four that genuinely cannot stand each other. Next time, somebody wins immunity, somebody makes fire, and somebody takes home a MILLION dollars. From all of us at the Aftermath — goodnight!

[Crowd roars. Lights down.]

[END OF AFTERMATH.]
